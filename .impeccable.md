## Design Context

### Users
Mixed audience - hiring managers, design peers, recruiters, collaborators, and friends. They're forming a first impression of Martta as a designer: her taste, her thinking, and whether they want to work with her. The site needs to reward both a quick scan and a longer browse.

### Brand Personality
**Minimal, restrained, human.** Quiet confidence - lets the work speak without shouting. Personal touches (Fufu the cat, swing dancing, sunflower favicon) add warmth without breaking the restraint. The site should feel like a well-edited room: nothing extra, everything intentional.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Editorial minimalism with warm undertones. Light mode only.
- **Typography**: Geist Sans (body, 400/500) + Tiempos Text (headlines, serif). Clean geometric paired with a warm serif - functional but not cold.
- **Palette**: Near-black ink (#1A1A1A), white surface, warm orange-red accent (#EC4523). Muted gray (#b0b0b0) for secondary text. No pure black, no pure white backgrounds.
- **Signature details**: Dotted underlines on links, numbered superscript badges, custom pixel cursor, interactive garden.
- **Anti-references**: No glassmorphism, gradient text, dark-mode glow, bounce easing, card grids, or anything that reads as "AI-generated portfolio." Keep it human and real.
- **Theme**: Light mode only - intentional choice, not a gap.

### Design Principles
1. **Nothing extra** - Every element earns its place. No decorative filler, no redundant copy, no features for features' sake.
2. **Quiet over loud** - Restraint is the aesthetic. Subtle hover states, gentle transitions, understated typography. Impact through precision, not volume.
3. **Human warmth** - The minimalism should feel warm, not sterile. Personal touches (Fufu, swing, sunflower) and the orange-red accent keep the palette from feeling corporate.
4. **Let the work lead** - The portfolio exists to showcase design work. Navigation, layout, and interaction should support browsing the projects, not compete with them.
5. **Accessible by default** - WCAG AA baseline: proper contrast, keyboard navigation, screen reader support, semantic HTML. Accessibility is not an afterthought.
